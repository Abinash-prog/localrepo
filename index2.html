Circulating DNA (ctDNA) Structure

Introduction

Circulating tumor DNA (ctDNA) is a component of cell-free DNA (cfDNA) that is shed from a tumor into the bloodstream. It has significant potential in cancer diagnostics and monitoring.

DNA Structure

The structure of DNA is a double helix formed by base pairs attached to a sugar-phosphate backbone.
